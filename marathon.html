Do Don't
  • Plan ahead
  • Decide to run the race at 10:30 the night before
  • Start a training regime several months in advance
  • Fail to run for more than 20 minutes at any point during the previous year
  • Have a substantial breakfast
  • Think that a croissant and a banana will be sufficient and start the race feeling hungry
  • Make careful plans for how to get to the start
  • Get off the commuter rail 3 miles from the start hoping to follow the masses and end up hitching a ride in a construction worker's pickup
  • Get to the start line with plenty of time to spare
  • Get stuck in line waiting for a portapotty and end up running to the start
  • Use lots of vaseline
  • Get runner's nipple
  • Arrange a precise spot for meeting people after the race
  • Spend 1 hour and 45 minutes looking for someone in a crowd of 20,000 people

    • Having said all this, failing to follow any of this advice will help to give the day an added sense of adventure. It certainly worked for me.
